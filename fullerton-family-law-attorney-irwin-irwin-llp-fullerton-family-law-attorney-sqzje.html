Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions we receive at our Fullerton office.

What does a Fullerton Family Law Attorney do?

A Fullerton Family Law Attorney at Irwin & Irwin LLP assists clients with legal matters involving family relationships. This includes guiding individuals through divorce, establishing child custody and support orders, drafting prenuptial agreements, and serving as minor's counsel in complex disputes.

How is child custody determined in California?

California courts determine child custody based strictly on the "best interests of the child." Judges will consider various factors, including the child's health, safety, welfare, and the nature of their emotional ties to each parent, to establish a stable and supportive living arrangement.

Do I need a lawyer for a divorce?

While you have the right to represent yourself, family law in California is highly complex. Having an experienced attorney ensures that your legal rights are protected, your paperwork is filed accurately, and you receive a fair settlement regarding asset division and support.

What are prenuptial agreements?

A prenuptial agreement is a legal contract entered into before marriage. It clearly outlines the division of assets, debts, and financial responsibilities in the event of a divorce or separation, providing peace of mind and financial protection for both parties.

What is minor's counsel?

Minor's counsel is an independent attorney appointed by the court to represent the best interests of a child in highly contested custody or visitation disputes. They ensure the child's voice is heard and considered by the judge without forcing the child to testify.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an MSB license in Canada?

An msb license, or Money Services Business registration, is a mandatory federal requirement administered by FINTRAC. It applies to businesses engaged in foreign exchange, money transferring, issuing or redeeming money orders, or dealing in virtual currencies.

Who needs to register with FINTRAC?

Any individual or entity operating a Money Services Business in Canada, or directing MSB services at persons or entities in Canada, must register with FINTRAC before commencing operations.

How long does the registration process take?

Once a complete and accurate application is submitted, FINTRAC typically processes registrations within a few weeks. However, the preparation of the required compliance program and risk assessment beforehand can take several weeks depending on the complexity of your business.

What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

Registered MSBs must maintain a comprehensive compliance program, appoint a compliance officer, verify client identities, keep specific records, and report suspicious and large cash/virtual currency transactions to FINTRAC.

Can Substance Law act as our Chief Compliance Officer?

Yes. We offer outsourced Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) services to help businesses manage their ongoing regulatory obligations, ensuring that your compliance program remains up-to-date with changing laws.

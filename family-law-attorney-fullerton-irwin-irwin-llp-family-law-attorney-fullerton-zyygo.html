Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a family law attorney in Fullerton?

You should look for an attorney with specific experience in California family law, a strong track record in local Orange County courts, and a communication style that makes you feel comfortable. At Irwin & Irwin LLP, we pride ourselves on being accessible, strategic, and deeply knowledgeable.

How is child custody determined in California?

In California, child custody is determined based strictly on the "best interests of the child." The court considers factors such as the child's health, safety, and welfare, as well as the nature and amount of contact with both parents. We help present your case to highlight your vital role in your child's life.

Do I need an attorney for an uncontested divorce?

While not legally required, having an attorney review an uncontested divorce agreement is highly recommended. A lawyer ensures that all paperwork is filed correctly, assets are divided fairly under California community property laws, and that you haven't unknowingly waived important rights.

What is minor's counsel?

Minor's counsel is an attorney appointed by the court to represent a child's best interests in highly contested custody cases. Our firm has specific expertise in this area, allowing us to deeply understand the nuances of complex custody disputes from all angles.

How long does the divorce process typically take?

California has a mandatory six-month waiting period from the date the respondent is served with divorce papers before the divorce can be finalized. However, complex cases involving significant assets or custody disputes can take much longer to resolve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions our clients ask. As leading Maricopa County family law attorneys, we aim to provide clarity and peace of mind during complex legal processes.

What areas of family law do you specialize in?

Sullivan Shick, PC specializes exclusively in Family Law, including divorce, legal separation, child custody, child support, spousal maintenance, modifications, and enforcement of orders within Maricopa County, Arizona.

How do I start a divorce proceeding in Maricopa County?

Starting a divorce involves filing a Petition for Dissolution of Marriage with the Maricopa County Superior Court. It's crucial to consult with an attorney to understand the specific requirements, timelines, and necessary documentation for your unique situation.

What is the difference between legal separation and divorce?

A legal separation allows couples to live apart and divide assets and debts without formally ending the marriage. Divorce, or dissolution of marriage, legally terminates the marital bond, allowing both parties to remarry. We can help you determine which path is right for you.

How is child custody determined in Arizona?

In Arizona, child custody (now referred to as "legal decision-making" and "parenting time") is determined based on the child's best interests. Factors considered include the child's wishes, parental fitness, and the relationship with each parent. Our attorneys will advocate strongly for your parental rights.

What should I bring to my initial consultation?

To make the most of your initial consultation, please bring any relevant documents such as marriage certificates, birth certificates of children, financial statements, property deeds, and any existing court orders. A detailed timeline of events can also be very helpful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our optometry practice and eye care services.

How often should I visit an Eye Doctor The Woodlands specialist?

We generally recommend a comprehensive eye exam every year for most patients. However, if you have specific eye conditions, wear contact lenses, have diabetes, or a family history of eye disease, our optometrists may suggest more frequent visits to ensure your eyes remain healthy.

Do you offer pediatric eye exams for children?

Absolutely. Early detection of vision issues is crucial for a child's development and learning. We provide gentle, comprehensive eye care for children, ensuring they have the visual skills necessary for success in school and everyday activities.

What eye diseases and conditions do you treat?

Our experienced optometrists diagnose and manage a wide range of eye conditions, including dry eye syndrome, glaucoma, macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, cataracts, and acute issues like eye infections or pink eye.

Can I bring my own prescription for glasses?

Yes! While we would love to be your primary eye care provider, you are always welcome to bring a valid, unexpired prescription from another doctor to purchase glasses from our extensive designer eyewear collection.

Do you accept walk-ins or do I need an appointment?

While we do our best to accommodate walk-ins and eye emergencies, we highly recommend scheduling an appointment to ensure minimal wait times and dedicated time with our eye doctors.
